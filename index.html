MysorePalace

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the most famous and historically significant landmarks in India. It is located in Mysore, Karnataka. The palace is renowned for its opulent architecture, rich history, and cultural significance. The Mysore Palace has a history dating back to the 14th century. Originally, it was a wooden palace built by the Wodeyar dynasty, the royal family of Mysore. The current Mysore Palace, a grand and Indo-Saracenic architectural masterpiece, was constructed between 1897 and 1912 after the old palace was gutted by a fire during a wedding ceremony in 1897. Maharaja Krishnaraja Wodeyar IV commissioned British architect Henry Irwin to design the new palace, blending Hindu, Mughal, Rajput, and Gothic styles. The result is a stunning blend of various cultural influences.

Varanasi Temple

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benares, is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities and is a major religious and cultural hub in India. It is closely associated with the Ganges River and is considered the holiest city in Hinduism. The city's temples are central to its religious identity, and some of them have deep historical significance.
